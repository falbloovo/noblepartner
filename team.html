Founders

The founders of Noble Partners have worked with each other for more than a decade, and were the earliest veterans in the Private Equity industry in the MENA, breaking ground in the early 2000s. Trusting relationship is one of the main drivers to make the firm successful and make sure collaborative team spirit is embedded within the firm culture and values. The founding partners closely manages and mentors every investment Noble Partners takes on.

While we boast an impressive investment portfolio, a strong network of partners, and a track record of excellence, our team of experts are the biggest asset of our firm. It is their experience and expertise, coupled with a collaborative spirit and unparalleled determination and ambition that make Noble Partners unique among Private Equity firms in the region.

Ammar possesses more than 24 years of private banking, private equity, and management consulting experience. He advised on several acquisitions across different sectors within the MENA region.

At Noble Partners, Ammar oversees all investment and divestment decisions made at the Firm, as well as the Firm’s strategic direction. Prior to founding Noble Partners, he was the head of private banking at BNP Paribas in Abu Dhabi, where he advised family offices and HNWIs on asset allocation and different investment opportunities including alternative investments, fixed income, structured products, derivatives, real estate and fund investments in the MENA region.

Before that, Ammar was a senior member of HSBC private banking group where he also advised some family offices on asset allocation and portfolio investments across different asset classes. Ammar also worked at the private banking group at Barclay in UAE.

Ammar serves as a Board Member of several entities within the GCC.

Education / Others

Ammar has lived in Bahrain and UAE. He worked across the Gulf Cooperation Council since late 1990s. He graduated from the American University of Beirut with a Bachelor Degree in Business Administration.

Iyad boasts more than 22 years of investing and strategy formulation experience spanning across multiple sectors such as Healthcare, Education, FMCG, Technology Logistics, Financial Services and other various sectors.

At Noble Partners, Iyad oversees all investment and divestment decisions made at the Firm, as well as the Firm’s strategic direction. Prior to founding Noble Partners, he was executive director at Al Masah Capital, a MENASEA private equity firm investing in various sectors including Healthcare, Education, Logistics etc. Before that, Iyad was a director of Kuwait based Global Capital Management, the MENA region private equity arm of Global Investment House. Iyad worked at Grant Thornton and KPMG in Kuwait in his earlier career.

Iyad serve as a board member of different leading companies in the MENASEA. He also served on the investment committee of the USD 650 million Global Buyout Fund of Global Capital Management.

Education / Others

Iyad has lived in Kuwait and later on relocated to Dubai, UAE. He has been working and transacting across the Gulf Cooperation Council since 2000. He graduated from Applied Science University in Amman with a B.S in business Administration, and earned his executive MBA from Cass Business School of City University of London.

Ahmad has more than 18 years of regional private equity experience, spanning across various industries and investment cycles.

At Noble Partners, Ahmad oversees all investment and divestment decisions made at the Firm, as well as the Firm’s strategic direction. Prior to founding Noble Partners, he was a Senior Director at Global Capital Management, a leading PE firm based out of Kuwait managing more than US$3 billion of assets. He served on the investment committee of the Global Opportunistic Fund II and on the board of leading companies in the MENA region.

Ahmad served as the Executive Chairman of Model Restaurants, a US$ 50 million listed company on Amman Stock Market, Vice Chairman of Al Rayan Holding Company in Kuwait, Board member in Sabaek Leasing & Investment Company in Kuwait, Board member in Al Jazeera Steel Products Company in Oman and other companies across the MENA region. Ahmad was instrumental in value creation, performance enhancement and investment exit. He was also responsible for advising on growth initiatives, management strategies, and organizational related structures. Prior to joining Global Capital Management, Ahmad worked in the Financial Advisory Group at KPMG.

Education / Others

Ahmad has lived in Kuwait, and worked across the Gulf Cooperation Council since early 2004. Ahmad holds a Bachelor Degree in Economics from University College London (UCL), Accounting & Finance Diploma from the London School of Economics (LSE), Master’s degree in Finance from the University of Leicester and a Doctorate from Manchester Business School in UK. He is also a CFA Level 3 Candidate.
